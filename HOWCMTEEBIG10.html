HOW WILL THE COMMITTEE HANDLE THE BIG TEN?

CHRIS CIMINO

We've already seen a fleury of upsets in what has to be one of the deepest leagues in the history of College Basketball. A team like Northwestern beats Michigan State and Ohio State, Maryland loses to Wisconsin, Minnesota has been on a tear. The conference is unpredictable and many of the top tier teams (Iowa, Wisconsin, Illinois, MSU, Michigan) are going to have a lot of losses. So this begs the question... what will the committee do?

Here is the bottom line- we know that Iowa, Wisconsin, Illinois, Michigan, Rutgers, Michigan State, Northwestern, Minnesota and Ohio State are in all likelihood heading to the NCAA Tournament. The conference will also have Indiana, Purdue, and Maryland contending for spots. If all of these teams beat up on one another, what does the committee do seeding wise? The answer is that they need to reward wins in this league much more than any other league. We saw Illinois (the 4th highest Big Ten team in the rankings) blast Duke (best ACC team?) a few weeks ago. We also saw MSU beat Duke, and the Spartans cannot buy a win in conference right now. We saw Penn State, who is probably 13th in the Big Ten, blow the doors off of a ranked Va. Tech team. Wisconsin destroyed a tournament level Louisville team. Iowa handled a good UNC team. The list goes on and on of the unbelievable performance of Big Ten teams in the non-conference.

The committee needs to look at these and acknowledge that if a team goes 13-7 and wins the league, they should be a one seed. Every night in the Big Ten is a nightmare, so a 13-7 Wisconsin team should get one seed looks over a 17-3 Kansas or Villanova because of the bye games that can happen in these other conferences that just do not exist in the Big Ten.
